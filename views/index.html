What is Catfacts.io?

Catfacts.io was a fun project I made to trial certain tools, and is based on the catfacts pranks that was popular while back. Only this implementation is less spammy, and you can unsubscribe at any time. If you like it and you want to contribute, just send me a pull request on github.

What happens when I subscribe?

When you enter your number in the field above you will be sent a confirmation text asking if it was really you that subscribed your own phone number. You can confirm or deny this by responding with Y or N. So you don't have to worry about being signed up without being aware of it, this also means if you decide subscribe your friend they will be notified.

Can I unsubscribe?

Once you subscribe you are told that, you can unsubscribe at any time by texting Q to catfacts. Once you text this, your number is removed from catfacts and you will no longer receive catfacts. Alternatively you unsubscribe right here from this site in the event twilio (the sms sevice providor) is not working. Just click unsubscribe in the top right corner, and enter your number.

Why am I no longer receving Catfacts?

If you are you no longer receving catfacts, This will likely mean catfacts has run out of twilio credit, and has stopped responding/sending out text messages. I may or may not put more credit on the account when it runs out. This will likely mean you can't unsubscribe via text message, but that's okay because you still do this via the site at the top of the page.

Can I trust Catfacts?

Catfacts is open source (MIT), meaning all it's code is freely avaible online for free use, extension, mirroring, critique, and whatever else. But most importantly it means catfacts is transparent in a way that you can be sure it's not doing any thing malicious with your phone number, if you have any suspicions. Just remember this isn't a serious project, so I don't plan take responsibility for any incidents, so be smart.
